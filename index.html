The Coastal Pollution Problem

Goa's coastline, famous for its golden sands and vibrant marine life, is facing a growing environmental crisis. Rapid urbanization, unchecked tourism, and industrial waste have increased pollution levels in both land and sea.Plastic debris litters beaches, untreated sewage contaminates nearshore waters, and oil spills threaten marine habitats. This not only disrupts the delicate balance of coastal ecosystems but also impacts local fishing communities and the tourism industry that Goa depends on. Without urgent action, the very charm and biodiversity that define Goa's shores risk being lost to pollution's long-lasting effects.
